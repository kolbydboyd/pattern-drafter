Why I built this

I sewed a garment from an AI-generated pattern. The fit was off and the instructions were terrible. So I tried drafting my own pattern from scratch. Turns out, pattern drafting is a mountain of math and it takes forever. All I wanted was a pair of shorts that fit me perfectly, made from good fabric, without spending a fortune.

So I built the engine behind People's Patterns. It uses the same parametric drafting formulas that professional pattern makers rely on, but you get your pattern in seconds and at a price that makes sense for home sewists.

Common questions

Patterns are generated using parametric drafting formulas, the same math taught in pattern-making courses. For most body proportions, the fit will be very close on the first try. We always recommend cutting a muslin (test garment in cheap fabric) first. If something is off, update your measurements and regenerate for free.

Most patterns need around 5 core measurements: chest or bust, waist, hips, inseam, and one or two lengths. The exact set depends on the garment. Each measurement has an on-screen guide showing you exactly where to place the tape.

No. Every pattern downloads as a tiled PDF sized for a standard home printer (US Letter or A4). Print at 100% scale, trim the edges, and tape the pages together. Each PDF includes a scale-check square and a numbered tile map so assembly is straightforward.
