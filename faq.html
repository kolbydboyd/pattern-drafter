Frequently Asked Questions

peoplespatterns.com

A made-to-measure pattern is drafted to your exact body measurements, not a standard size. You enter your measurements once, and we generate a pattern that accounts for your actual waist, hip, inseam, chest, and other dimensions. The result fits your body, not a size chart.

Most sewing patterns are drafted for a statistical average body in standard sizes (XS, S, M, L, XL). If your measurements don't match that average (and most people's don't), you have to make alterations before the pattern fits. Our patterns skip that step entirely. Every pattern is generated fresh from your measurements.

Patterns are generated using parametric drafting formulas - the same math taught in pattern-making courses. For most body proportions, the fit will be very close out of the box. We always recommend cutting a muslin (test garment in cheap fabric) first. This is standard practice even with professionally drafted patterns. If something is off, update your measurements and re-generate for free.

Every pattern includes: print-ready tiled PDF (US Letter and A4), full materials list with fabric type and yardage, stitch and notions guide, step-by-step numbered construction instructions, and a scale verification page. All drafted to your exact measurements.

Each pattern includes a complete materials list, fabric recommendations, stitch settings, and numbered construction steps written in plain language with sewing terms explained inline. Beginner-friendly patterns are marked. We recommend starting with the T-Shirt or A-Line Skirt if you're new to garment sewing.

After purchase, you download a tiled PDF sized to standard printer paper (US Letter, A4, or Tabloid). Print at 100% scale; never "fit to page." Each page includes a 2x2 inch verification square to confirm your print is the right scale, plus registration crosshairs to align the pages when taping them together. Full assembly instructions are included on page 2 of every PDF.

Each tile page has scissors cut lines on the edges and crosshair alignment marks. Cut along the scissors line on the incoming tile, align the crosshairs with the adjacent tile, and tape from the back. A numbered tile map is included showing how the pages fit together. Full instructions are on page 2 of every PDF.

US Letter (8.5×11″) works for most home printers in the US. A4 works for most international printers. Tabloid (11×17″) reduces the number of pages significantly if your printer supports it. A0 prints on a single large sheet at any copy shop, or use it as a projector file - no taping required.
