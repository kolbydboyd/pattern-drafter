# People's Patterns — Video Content Ladder

13 Videos Across 3 Funnel Rungs | YouTube + TikTok/Reels/Shorts

---

## The Ladder Strategy

| Rung | Mission | Content Type | CTA Type | Audience |
|------|---------|--------------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Problem awareness | Educational, relatable | Soft (subscribe, comment) | Broad |
| 2 | Solution awareness | Demonstrative, Process | Medium (visit site, try free) | Narrowing |
| 3 | Product awareness | Product-led, social proof | Direct (generate, buy) | Targeted |

Mental model: Every Rung 1 video earns the right to make Rung 2 and 3 more effective. Viewers who click on a Rung 3 video directly may not convert — but the viewer who climbed from Rung 1 has the context that makes the pitch land. That's why the ladder exists.

## RUNG 1: Problem Awareness

_The sell: there is no sell._

### R1-V1 — Why Your Clothes Never Fit Right

- **Hook:** "You're not hard to fit. You're being sold clothes that fit no one."
- **Content:** Explains that RTW isn't designed to fit your body — it's designed to fit the most bodies possible at the lowest possible cost. Shows how sizes are determined differently by every brand. Standard sizing was invented in WWII for uniforms — not for your body.
- **Results:** The viewer feels validated. No product pitch.
- **CTA:** Have you ever found a garment that actually fits perfectly? Tell me in the comments.

### R1-V2 — The Real Cost of Cheap Clothes

- **Hook:** "This $15 shirt costs way more than $15."
- **Content:** Breaks down the real cost of fast fashion — garment workers, environmental waste, clothes that fall apart after a few washes. Not preachy or guilt-driven. Let the numbers do the talking.
- **Results:** Connects consumer behavior to consequences without moralizing.
- **CTA:** How much do you spend on clothing per year? Be honest.

### R1-V3 — The Pareto Tip in Your Closet

- **Hook:** "Same shirt. Same fabric. Different journey."
- **Content:** 80/20 rule applied to your wardrobe — you wear 20% of your closet 80% of the time. The other 80% sits there. Quick exercise: go through your closet and count. It's not about having fewer things — it's about making the right ones.
- **Results:** Gives a memorable framework. Plants the seed of intentional wardrobe building.
- **CTA:** Do your own 80/20 count and share your ratio in the comments.

### R1-V4 — 5 Reasons You Should Learn to Sew

- **Hook:** "Sewing is not your grandmother's hobby." _(Full script in 10-video-script-learn-to-sew.md)_
- **Content:** Five reasons framed as practical advantages, not craft enthusiasm: fit, cost per wear, knowledge gap, pricing double standard, and creative freedom. Each reason is a standalone argument — no fluff.
- **Results:** Reframes sewing from hobby to practical skill. Viewer reconsiders assumptions.
- **CTA:** If you've been curious about sewing but haven't started — tell me what's holding you back.

### R1-V5 — The Real Cost of Sewing Your First Garment vs. Your Fifth

- **Hook:** "Your first sewing project costs $150. Your fifth costs $15. Here's why."
- **Content:** Breaks down how the upfront cost of sewing (machine, tools, fabric, mistakes) amortizes over time. First garment = expensive. But by garment five, you already own the tools, you waste less fabric, and your cost per garment drops dramatically. Compare to buying five RTW garments at the same quality level.
- **Results:** Flips the "sewing is expensive" objection. Shows the compounding return.
- **CTA:** Are you on your first garment or your fifth? Tell me in the comments.

## RUNG 2: Solution Awareness

_Now we talk about sewing._

### R2-V1 — How Custom Sewing Patterns Actually Work

- **Hook:** "A pattern is just math that turns your body into a garment."
- **Content:** Explains what a sewing pattern is, how custom/made-to-measure patterns differ from off-the-rack commercial patterns. Shows a high-level walkthrough — you input measurements, an algorithm calculates the shapes, you get printable pieces. Demystifies the process without showing a specific product.
- **CTA:** Visit site to learn more. Link in description.

### R2-V2 — I Made Pants That Actually Fit for $11 in Fabric

- **Hook:** "These pants were $11 and they fit better than anything in my closet."
- **Content:** Full cost breakdown of making a pair of pants from scratch — fabric ($11), thread ($1), zipper ($2), pattern (free/$9–14). Total under $25. Compare to $60–100 for similar quality RTW. Show the finished garment, try it on, talk about the fit difference. This is a proof-of-concept video.
- **CTA:** Direct link to site.

### R2-V3 — What is a Muslin and Why Every Sewist Makes One

- **Hook:** "What if you could test-drive your clothes before you commit?"
- **Content:** Explains what a muslin/toile is — a test version of a garment in cheap fabric. Why every serious sewist makes one first. Shows the process: cut muslin, sew it up, try on, check fit, make adjustments, then cut the real fabric. Frames it as quality control, not extra work.
- **CTA:** Save this for when you're starting your first sewing project.

### R2-V4 — Why Made-to-Measure (Brief) and Why It's Coming Back

- **Hook:** "Made to MTM... let's put the history into context for you."
- **Content:** Brief history of made-to-measure — how all clothing was custom before industrialization, how RTW took over, and why technology is now enabling a return to custom fit at accessible prices. Not nostalgic — forward-looking. Connects historical craftsmanship with modern tools.
- **CTA:** Link in description to learn more about modern made-to-measure options.

## RUNG 3: Product Awareness

_Now we show the tool._

### R3-V1 — I Built an App That Drafts Patterns From Your Measurements

- **Hook:** "What if you could type in your measurements and get a print-ready pattern in 30 seconds?"
- **Content:** Screen recording walkthrough of People's Patterns — enter measurements, select garment, customize options, generate pattern, download PDF. Show the full flow from input to printed and assembled pattern. Keep it tight, no filler. This is the demo video.
- **CTA:** Try it free. Link in description.

### R3-V2 — Making Cargo Shorts From a Custom Pattern (Full Process)

- **Hook:** "Custom fit cargo shorts from tap to measurements to finished garment."
- **Content:** Full sew-along from start to finish. Measure, generate in the app, print tiles, assemble, lay out on fabric, cut, and sew. Show every major step. Honest about challenges (this seam was tricky, I repinned here). End with the finished garment on body. This is the flagship "proof it works" video.
- **Results:** Viewer sees the entire journey. Builds trust. Demonstrates that the patterns actually produce wearable garments.
- **CTA:** The link to generate your own cargo shorts pattern is in the description.

### R3-V3 — Why I Price Men's and Women's Patterns the Same

- **Hook:** "Same construction, same materials, same price. Always."
- **Content:** Explains the pink tax in pattern and fashion pricing. Shows that the typical premium on women's patterns and clothing has no basis in construction cost. People's Patterns charges the same regardless of gender. Not a sales pitch — a values statement that naturally highlights the product.
- **Results:** Viewers who care about fair pricing feel aligned with the brand.
- **CTA:** Try it for yourself. See that the price is the same for everyone.

### R3-V4 — Sew Along: Button Down Shirt From Custom Pattern

- **Hook:** "Let's build a shirt that actually fits."
- **Content:** Detailed step-by-step sew-along for a button-down shirt. Collar stand, placket, cuffs, set-in sleeves — all the techniques that intimidate beginners, broken down clearly. This is the most complex sew-along and proves the patterns handle advanced garments. Film in long sessions, edit tight.
- **CTA:** Get the pattern for free — link in description. Subscribe for more sew-alongs.
