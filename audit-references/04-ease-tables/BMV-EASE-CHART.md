# BMV Ease Chart — Vogue / Butterick / McCall

Source: Vogue Patterns, Butterick, McCall (published on pattern-house websites)  
Industry de-facto standard for **woven** fabric ease.

Values are total DESIGN ease (above body measurement). "Fit at waist/hip" is
typically 1" less than bust on fitted styles.

---

## Blouses / Shirts / Dresses — Bust Ease

| Fit Category | Total ease at bust |
|---|---|
| Close-fitting | 0–2⅞" |
| Fitted | 3–4" |
| Semi-fitted | 4⅛–5" |
| Loose-fitting | 5⅛–8" |
| Very loose | over 8" |

## Pants / Skirts — Hip Ease

| Fit Category | Total ease at hip |
|---|---|
| Close-fitting | 0–2" |
| Fitted | 2–3" |
| Semi-fitted | 3–4" |
| Loose-fitting | 4–6" |
| Very loose | over 6" |

## Jackets (worn over blouse/dress — add ~2" to dress category)

| Fit Category | Bust ease |
|---|---|
| Fitted | 3¾–4⅓" |
| Semi-fitted | 4⅓–6" |
| Loose | 6–10" |
| Very loose | over 10" |

## Coats (worn over jacket — add ~2" to jacket category)

| Fit Category | Bust ease |
|---|---|
| Fitted | 5⅓–6¾" |
| Semi-fitted | 6¾–8" |
| Loose | 8–12" |
| Very loose | over 12" |

## Menswear Shirt Ease (sewguide.com)

| Fit | Ease |
|-----|------|
| Extreme slim | 3.5" / 9 cm |
| Slim | 4¾" / 12 cm |
| Normal | 6¼" / 16 cm |
| Loose | 7¾" / 20 cm |

## People's Patterns UPPER_EASE vs BMV Chart

| PP label | PP ease | BMV category |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| fitted | +2" | Between Close-fitting and Fitted |
| standard | +4" | Fitted (3–4") ✓ |
| relaxed | +6" | Loose-fitting (5⅛–8") ✓ |
| boxy | +8" | Upper end of Loose-fitting ✓ |
| oversized | +10" | Very loose (over 8") ✓ |

Note: PP "fitted" at +2" sits in the BMV close-fitting range, not the "Fitted"
range. This is intentional for MTM (zero sizing-tolerance buffer needed), but
may feel tight to users expecting BMV "Fitted" ease of 3–4".

---

## Caveats

- Ease "industry standards" do not exist. BMV, Aldrich, Armstrong, and Müller & Sohn
  all use different baseline ease values.
- This chart applies to **wovens only**. For knits, use KNIT-STRETCH-FORMULA.md.
- MTM patterns should use LESS design ease than RTW because there is no
  sizing-tolerance buffer needed. See MTM-WEARING-EASE-MINIMUMS.md.
