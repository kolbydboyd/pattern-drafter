Source: muellerundsohn.com (free construction articles in English)  
This is the closest free equivalent to a European pattern-cutting textbook.

## Collar Formulas

### Stand-Up Collar
- Collar line length = neckline length − 0.5 cm (fits close to neck)
- To flare away from neck: INCREASE the CB seam length

### Mandarin / Band Collar
- Lower neckline: 1 cm at shoulder, 0.5 cm at CB, 1.5 cm at CF
- Collar height: 3.5–4.5 cm

### Shirt Collar with Stand (Two-Piece)
Draft collar height in 3 segments:
| Segment | Height |
|---------|--------|
| Stand | 4.5 cm |
| Roll | 4.5 cm |
| Fall | 5.5 cm |

### Convertible (One-Piece) Collar
- Rectangle: height = stand + fall (typically 3–4")
- Length = half neckline (front neck + back neck measured at seam line)

### Standard Shirt Collar Dimensions
- Stand: ~1" (2.5 cm)
- Gap: ~2" (5 cm)
- Fall: ~1.5" (4 cm)

### Notch / Men's Tailored Collar
- Stand: 1⅛–1¼"
- Fall: 1⅝–1¾"
- Shift: ½–⅞" (depends on size and break point)

## Trouser Block

Published formulas on muellerundsohn.com cover:
- Crotch depth = waist-to-seat measurement
- Seat width = seat/4 + ease per panel
- CB raise: 1.5–2 cm

## Reference Context

The German drafting tradition (Müller & Sohn) uses different proportion rules for:
- Armhole depth (generally similar to Aldrich at chest/8 + constant)
- Sleeve cap height (uses armscye circumference, not depth)
- Collar dimensions (slightly larger stand height than US/UK conventions)

The freely published articles are the most complete free textbook-quality source
for collar construction. Use them as ground truth for `collar`, `shirt-collar`,
and `mandarin` pieces in the audit.
