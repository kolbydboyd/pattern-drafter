# Armstrong — Patternmaking for Fashion Design (5th ed.)

Source: Helen Joseph-Armstrong, *Patternmaking for Fashion Design* (5th ed., Pearson)  
Free access: Internet Archive controlled lending — archive.org/details/patternmakingfor0000jose_q3g3

---

## Three Core Principles

All design patterns derive from one or more of:

1. **Dart manipulation** — rotating, combining, or transferring darts to achieve
   different silhouettes while preserving fit. A dart is a triangular fold sewn into
   fabric to accommodate 3D body curves.

2. **Added fullness** — introducing gathers, tucks, pleats, or flare to create
   volume beyond what the body requires.

3. **Contouring** — using shaped seams, darts, or boning to mold the fabric to the
   body's 3D form.

---

## Basic Sloper Set

Armstrong derives all design patterns from a basic sloper set:
- Bodice front + back
- Skirt front + back
- Sleeve
- Torso (combined bodice)
- Pant

---

## Bodice Block — Key Relationships

Proportions broadly similar to Aldrich; specific constants differ slightly.

### Ease at block (close-fit reference):
- Bust: 3–4" total (slightly more than Aldrich's 2" = 5 cm per side)
- Waist: 1–2" total

### Dart Intake
Armstrong's total dart intake at waist = difference between bust ease block
width and waist ease block width. Standard distribution:
- Side seam dart: largest portion
- Front waist dart: medium
- Back waist dart: medium
- Optional shoulder dart: small

---

## Sleeve Cap

Armstrong includes 1–1½" cap ease as standard for woven set-in sleeves.

Cap height rule (Armstrong): approximately ½ the armscye depth (measured as
the distance from the shoulder-to-underarm at the side seam line).

---

## Special Sleeve Types

Armstrong covers structurally distinct sleeve types with their own block logic:
- **Set-in**: standard cap height, cap ease 0.5–1.5"
- **Raglan**: no cap; sleeve incorporates shoulder section; bodice loses shoulder
  mass to sleeve piece
- **Dropped shoulder**: armhole extended 2–8 cm below natural shoulder point;
  cap height reduced by drop amount; sleeve becomes wider and flatter
- **Kimono**: sleeve and bodice cut as one piece; armhole replaced by straight line;
  minimal cap shaping (Bray's kimono-block chapter is the best free reference)

---

## Pant Block

Similar to Aldrich in overall approach; specific crotch curve shape differs.

Key formulas:
- Crotch depth = seated waist-to-floor minus floor-to-seat = `crotchDepth` measurement
- Back rise tilt: ~1.5–2 cm CB raise to accommodate seat curve
- Front extension: ¼ of front panel hip width
- Back extension: ½ of back panel hip width

## Textbook Authority

Armstrong is widely used in US fashion education. Where it agrees with Aldrich
and FreeSewing, treat the formula as cross-validated. Where all three agree, flag
any People's Patterns deviation as a **DEFECT**.
